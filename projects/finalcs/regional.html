Regional Vietnamese Specialties

Vietnam's long geography and diverse climate create three distinct culinary regions, each with unique flavors and cooking techniques. From the subtle flavors of the North to the spicy dishes of Central Vietnam and the sweet, herbaceous cuisine of the South, regional differences make Vietnamese food endlessly fascinating.

Northern Cuisine

Northern Vietnamese food is mild and not very spicy. The flavors are light and natural. Because the weather is cooler, fewer spices grow there. People often use black pepper instead of chili. The dishes focus on the true taste of the ingredients.

Central Cuisine

Hue's royal food is fancy and beautiful. Central Vietnamese food is very spicy, with bold flavors from shrimp paste and chili. Meals usually include many small side dishes.

Southern Cuisine

Southern Vietnamese food is sweet and fresh, with lots of herbs. It uses coconut milk, sugar, and many greens. The warm weather means plenty of fruits and veggies all year. It has some flavors from Cambodia and Thailand.
