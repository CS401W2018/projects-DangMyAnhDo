Vietnamese Coffee Culture

Vietnam is the world's second-largest coffee producer and has developed a unique coffee culture that blends French colonial influences with local ingenuity. From the slow-drip phin filter to the famous egg coffee, Vietnamese coffee offers distinctive flavors and rituals.

Signature Vietnamese Coffee Styles

1. Cà phê đá (Iced Coffee)

The classic Vietnamese iced coffee made with:

  • Dark roast Vietnamese coffee beans
  • Traditional metal phin filter
  • Sweetened condensed milk
  • Pour over ice

2. Cà phê trứng (Egg Coffee)

Hanoi's famous creamy invention:

  • Egg yolks whipped with condensed milk
  • Poured over strong black coffee
  • Created during milk shortages in the 1940s
  • Served hot or iced

Reference: Vietnamese Coffee Styles

How to Brew Vietnamese Coffee at Home

Traditional Phin Brewing Method

  1. Add 2-3 tbsp of coarse ground coffee to the phin
  2. Place the press on top and add 20ml hot water (90-95°C)
  3. Wait 30 seconds for the coffee to bloom
  4. Fill the phin with water and cover with lid
  5. Wait 4-5 minutes for complete dripping
  6. Stir in condensed milk to taste

Reference: Nguyen Coffee Supply Brewing Guide
