Iconic Vietnamese Street Food

Vietnamese street food is the lifeblood of the country's culinary culture. From dawn until late at night, streets across Vietnam come alive with food vendors serving quick, delicious, and affordable meals. Here are some must-try street foods:

Popular Street Food Items

Bánh mì

The famous Vietnamese baguette sandwich, with French colonial roots but distinctly Vietnamese flavors. A perfect banh mi contains:

  • Crispy baguette
  • Pâté and mayonnaise
  • Cold cuts or grilled meats
  • Pickled vegetables
  • Cilantro and chili

Regional variations include different meats and additional toppings like fried eggs or seafood.

Bún chả

Hanoi's signature dish features grilled pork served over rice noodles with herbs and dipping sauce. Key elements:

  • Caramelized grilled pork patties
  • Thin slices of grilled pork belly
  • Rice vermicelli noodles
  • Fresh herbs (mint, cilantro, perilla)
  • Sweet-sour fish sauce broth

Often served with crispy spring rolls on the side.

Bánh xèo

These crispy turmeric pancakes are a Southern street food favorite:

  • Rice flour and coconut milk batter
  • Turmeric for golden color
  • Fillings of pork, shrimp, and bean sprouts
  • Served with lettuce and herbs for wrapping
  • Dipped in fish sauce

Named "xèo" for the sizzling sound when cooking.
